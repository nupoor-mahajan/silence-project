Interpreting the Analysis

How to read the Silence Map and Alert Signals

1. Reading the Silence Map

The Scatter Plot visualizes the relationship between Population Size (x-axis) and Complaint Volume (y-axis).

Standard Reporting

Regions appearing as Blue Dots are behaving normally. Their complaint volume matches their population size relative to the average baseline.

Flagged Silence

Regions appearing as Orange/Red Dots are statistical anomalies. They have significantly fewer complaints than expected for their population density.

2. Key Metrics Explained

Metric Definition & Interpretation
Complaint Density Number of complaints per 1,000 citizens. This normalizes data to compare villages with cities fairly.
Mean Density The average reporting rate across the dataset. This establishes the "Baseline of Normalcy."
Z-Score Statistical calculation of "how weird is this silence?" Higher scores indicate intentional suppression.

3. Status Indicators

The ranked list provides two specific types of status alerts:

Low Signal

Meaning: Reporting is low, but we lack historical data to confirm why.

Action: Monitor the region for future patterns and data accumulation.

⚠️ Trend Gap (Fear Indicator)

Meaning: This region used to report frequently, but has suddenly dropped to near zero.

Implication: Strongly suggests suppression or broken reporting infrastructure.
